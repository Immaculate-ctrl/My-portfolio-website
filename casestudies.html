Project Description

Designing a restaurant brand identity inspired by either the African, Oceania or Americana cultures.

Name Explanation

Asanti: A name that means because of war in Twi but I actually used the meaning from the Kiswahili language which means "Thank you" or "gratitude." Maison: French for: "House" or "home."

Asante Maison is a royal house of Ghanaian heritage where people gather to experience the culture, cuisine, and traditions of the Asante people through fine dining.

Who We Are

Asante Maison exists to preserve, celebrate, and share the spirit of Ghana through unforgettable dining experiences. Our values include heritage, gathering, community, celebration and royalty. We want every customer to feel like they are a part of us, to be treated like royalty and to enjoy an amazing company around the restaurant.

We have decided to tell stories even through every dish.

Our Story

Asante Maison is a royal house of Ghanaian heritage that is inspired by the culture and heritage of the Asante people. We offer more than a meal, we offer storytelling through our menus/cuisines, good hospitality and a treatment fit for royalty. We want people to feel at home and learn about the roots of the Asante people through our visuals, staff, music, and cuisine.

Purpose

To celebrate, and share the heritage, cuisine, and hospitality of the Asante people through fine dining experiences.

Mission

To give our guests traditional flavours and excite them with the Asante stories while giving them an exceptional fine dining experience with excellence and cultural pride.

Vision

To become the leading destination for authentic Ghanaian fine dining.

Values

Uncompromised material honesty, architectural geometric precision, and timeless spatial endurance variables.

Personality

Brand Archetype: The Ruler + Care Giver - we give you a royal treatment, fine dining, and treat you with dignity. We welcome you warmly, offer good service, and a sense of community. Asante Maison welcomes you with warmth, treats you like royalty, and shares the amazing heritage of Asante people through fine dining experience.

Brand Personality: Sophisticated Cultured Hospitable Proud Refined

Logo Concepts

The first concept was developed from the soul washer (one of the gold pieces of the Asante culture). The soul washers are only worn by priests or officials that are around the King who perform purification rituals for the King. They symbolize purity and magical protection.

The second concept is inspired ny their golden stool. The golden stool known as Sika Dwa Kofi is said to have descended in a white cloud dust from the heaven and fell on the lap of Osei Tutu the first and housed the soul of the nation. No one sits or touches the stool, it is even given its own chair to sit on in ceremonies. When Kings are crowned, they are lifted over and lowered on the other side of the stool without touching it. It represents the soul of the nation

Logo Concepts

The icon is inspired by the Golden Stool. Representing the Asante people. The symbol is flat to give it a modern look. The edges give it that “Ruler” personality behind it. We made sure that it is not too sharp to scare people away.

Wormark

The font choice is Proxima Nova Alt condensed. The font is tall and evenly spaced - giving the brand a luxurious look and feel. I have then rounded the corners a bit to add a soft friendly feel to it.

Logo in black and white

Other symbols used

The symbol originates from the leaf like elements in the Akrafokonmu symbol. The Akrafokonmu symbol is the known as the soul washer - it was worn by people who were high in rank and were around the King. They would serve the King and purify and replenish his vital powers. The leaf like elements are linked directly to the Adinkra symbol Aya which endurance, resourcefulness, independence, and defiance against adversity and it serves as a visual reminder to adapt, flourish and remain strong through life’s hardship.

Other symbols used

I then chose to make these symbols represent different departments in the restaurant. We have a menu symbol, waiter symbol, chef symbol, food symbol and since our name includes a French word, I also have added French cuisines on the menu.

Our pattern

Colours

The colours used are directly form the Kente cloth. They are muted to make them feel premium and sophisticated.

RESTAURANT IDENTITY

Project Description

Label and bottle design for a premium sparkling water brand. Later in the stage it was decided to add Vodka and Gin Bottle designs.

Who We Are

AquaElite in its naming is self-explanatory. This is water curated for the Elite ("a choice or select body, the best part,"1823, from French élite). So, this water brand is for the rich and wealthy - the selected few that are considered the best.

Our Story

The company was started by myself (Gift Thabanyane) and four gentlemen. We are a group and call ourselves The Immacculates. I decided to start this after I read and discovered that water from the tap had contaminants. They then started the research as to where we could find fresh, clean, drinking water and we discovered the Alliston aquifer. Alliston aquifer is the ancient underground reservoir located under the Simcoe Uplands in Tiny, Springwater, Oro-Medonte and Tay Townships (Canada). Top Scientists have referred to the water in Alliston aquifer as the purest wateron the planet. It has become the benchmark for water purity around the world. This is where the journey started..

Vision

Be the standard of the purest water on the planet.

Values

EXCLUSIVITY - men that are of LSM 7+.
LUXURY - a luxurious looking packaging.
PURITY - from the Alliston aquifer.
HEALTH - our concern is the health of our customers. Improving the quality of their health through our water is our #1 priority.
STRENGTH - our water bottle design sourced inspiration from building to showcase strength.

Moodboard

This board serves as a directional approach that I wanted to take in designing the logo for AquaElite. The font had to be bold; with inspiration taken from the Voss typography. I wanted to use the water molecular substances and other molecular substances found in sparkling water. This approach did not work well conceptually but we will discuss this in the logo conception stage.

Logo Concepts

Here I have explored a range of things; from water droplets, letter pairing, and using purely typography of which did not work. I think my mind was not fixed on an idea and I was just wondering all around the place and hence nothing seemed to be working.

Selected concept

The icon I decided to go with was actually what we call a “happy mistake”. This is on the bottom left. I actually meant to make an A and from that A make a flow of water as if water is flowing from the centre of an A. I looked at it after sketching it and saw AE and immediately I said “that’s it”. It is a unique letter combination that gives someone a smile in the mind when they recognize it. It is less complex and stands out from the rest of the concepts.

Word mark choice

The font used for the word mark is called Montrelo. Montrelo looks modern, it is a sans serif font with rounded edges. Complimented the icon very well. It is bold and aligned with our brands values. The font is paired with poppins; tracked for spacing in between.

Logo in black and white

These are the final logo designs in black and white.

The bottle design Conceptualization

The bottle took inspiration from the Leonardo hotel in Sandton. The reason was to create a strong glass bottle that people will be able to reuse after drinking the water and made to be easy to carry around. Taking the building design made sense for this aspect. Also, this is a five-star hotel where I would imagine out target market would stay in and the water could even be sold in such a place.

The bottle label design Conceptualization

These are the back and front of the water bottle labels. Since the bottle is glass, I wanted nothing to interfere with seeing the water inside, so the logo will be embossed on the bottle and the label at the back will be a transparent sticky plastic with writing on it.

Flavours label design Conceptualization

Introducing the labels was quite a challenge. So, I decided to have a small rubber band type of a description that will be simple, clean and not interfere with a lot. The one I decided to go with is on the far right.

Project Description

Logo design, perfume bottle design and packaging design for an Asian inspired brand.

Who We Are

The temple Wat Arun (in Thailand; Bangkok) is name after Aruna who is the god of the sun in Hindu cosmology. The temple is a tourist attraction; it is a spiritual place and therefore it is respected. People dress well when going there. It is also adored for it's iridescence at dawn or sunset when the sun is heating upon it because it is made of ceramics and sea shells mosaics. Aruna was the one who protected the earth from the sun's harmful rays and only allowed essential light for survival to pass through.

Purpose

What we aim to do at Aruna is to produce scents with purpose. What we mean is that just as Aruna had a purpose to protect, we also have designed a scent(s) for those that are purpose driven. Dawn has a spiritual significance and that is the notion of illumination and hope, the beginning of a new day and thus a chance for improvement and impact. Sunrise is symbolic for birth, rebirth or awakening. So, Aruna is designed for individuals that are change agents, when they appear on the scene people have hope - they are like a light in the darkness, they always seek to conquer themselves or surpass the achievements of yesterday and they always want to impact other people positively. Dawn’s impact on individuals brings mindful awareness and activates a portal of divine wisdom. So, these are people who always seek wisdom - these are creators.

Mission

We aim to offer perfumes that do not harm the skin, that demand attention. Our stories and messaging are constructed to capture the right people to buy our perfumes. We ignite focus and confidence with our stories and messaging.

Vision

We want to create a loyal community of individuals that carry these traits and characteristics. We want Aruna to be known as a brand that is worn by such people and we want such people to connect to the brand. Individuals that are full of light, positive mindset and wisdom. Just as Aruna would protect the earth and only allow the essential light to pass, these are individuals that filter the unnecessary noise that is in the world and only let the positive, uplifting and inspiring voices to be heard in their lives and upon those whom they are raising.

Values

Exclusivity | Elegance Purpose | Minimalism | Integrity.

Personality

Sophisticated | Spiritual Inspiring | Mysterious | Refined.

Logo concepts

The logo concepts revolve around the horses, the mosaic, the sun, and the temple of dawn. This is because the focus of the research was on the culture found in Bangkok (Thailand). I was looking at the temple of Dawn as an iconic and most visited or famous spiritual place in Bangkok. Wat Aruna (which is the temple of Dawn) gets its name from Aruna who was the charioteers in Hindu cosmology. Aruna was riding 7 horses and was considered god of the sun. The temple itself is made of a mosaic of sea shells which gives it an amazing iridescence when the sun hits on it. Hence the concepts revolve around that.

Chosen concept

The icon chosen is the one with the building negative space in the middle of a circular shape and mosaic inside the circle and the sun rays. Top right

Logo wordmark

The Aruna wordmark is a key element to the brand’s visual identity. Designed to be clean, modern, luxurious and reflects the brands core values of exclusivity, quality, excellence, elegance and care. The typography has been carefully selected to convey elegance, modernity, and luxury. Bringing heritage into our modern world.

Icon Makeup

The icon is made of a combination of a mosaic (representing the temple’s decorations), the mosaic is in a circular form (representing the sun), the building of the temple in a negative space inside the circle, and the 7 sun rays representing the seven horses that Aruna used to ride.

Colours

Colours come from the scent types we have.
First one being an aquatic scent: op notes:
Musk, ozonic notes, sea algae – fresh marine note with soft woody and vanilla scent. Middle notes: Lily, Amber – mixture of fresh green floral scent. Base notes: Marine, dutura – fresh, aquatic, cold, salty, and earthy scent.

And the sweet woody scent:
Top notes: Pear, ginger, lavender, ylang-ylang – Delightful fruity scent of sweetness. Middle notes: Pink pepper, cinnamon, amber, ciste labdanum – sweet and spicy scent. Bottom notes: vanilla, tonka bean, agarwood, patchouli, cypriol – sweet smell with a strong woody spicy scent.

Logo in Black and White

The concepts I have revolved around the horses, mosaic, the temple, the logo icon, and iridescence. For the first one in the blue box, I was doing the design for the aquatic scent. I used the logo icon as the structure for the bottle. The front structure would look like that one that is in blues and black. Other structures around it are how it will look on the sides. The issue with this one was the way the word mark was sitting at the centre, that a person reading had to turn their heard to read it.

The one in the orange box was for the sweet scented perfume. Here I went for the use of the face of horses and the iridescence on the sides of it. The cap of it was also going to be the head of the horse. It was not the best, the structure of it was too basic.

The third one in the green box I wanted it to be a double scented with detachable glasses that contain the perfume. The word mark was showing very well there at the bottom, but what was not showing was the temple in the middle. I did not want it to show personalty because I wanted the structure to look more stable to the eye and I was also thinking of when one is detached, the other one will not fall off, the entire structure will remain stable. I had to change it and make the temple show and also make the bottles not to be detachable as well. Which lead to the designs on the next page.

Illustrator Designs

Started to do illustrator designs for the perfume bottles before going to the 3D modeling phase.

This is the middle part of the perfume (Wat Aruna).

This is made of stainless steel material that has some texture to it. The stainless steel is chosen because it makes the bottle look more luxurious. It is more durable and stable. So it will provide that sense of stability for the middle structure and will be able to hold the perfume bottle in place in case the other half of the bottle finishes first. It lasts even longer and does not degrade over time.

Circular structure

This is where the perfume is contained. There is a separation in the middle to separate the two scents from mixing. This container is made out of a thick glass. One is able to see the inside but it is not really clear.

The “mosaic” are windows on the glass where you are able to see the perfume clearly. These are not coloured, they show the perfume inside, which is the one that is coloured.

Glass is good for preventing the perfume from evaporating and also prevents it from being contaminated. It provides a perfect seal that lasts over time. Also it has a luxurious look and feel.

The Actuator & nozzle

The actuator (bottom) is the button you press to release the perfume. The nozzle (top) is what dispenses the perfume. Both are made of glass metal. The nozzle for the oil one is a bit wider to avoid the oil from clogging.

Project Description

Design a logo, corporate identity, promotional campaign and branding for a 3-Day Jazz event: Soweto Jazz Festival/Sophia-town Jazz Festival. The logo has to reflect ideas of jazz music, and a festive feeling.

What Is Jazz?

Jazz cannot be defined because it is always evolving. This music was started by African Americans. It has an element of improvisation - doing something that was not initially intended, planned or practiced. So Jazz is spontaneous.

Jazz is enjoyed by creative people, intellectuals and introspective people. These people enjoy abstract thinking - that is thinking and understanding things that are not tangible like freedom, justice, love, etc., and always want to experience new things. They are emotional beings and are good problem solvers.

Logo Concepts

These were the initial concept conceived but they were not the best. They did resemble some of the logos that are already there and felt old some of them and something that feels young and festive was needed.

Logo design

This is where I ended at and pay attention to how we got here.
First a font called Bebas Neue Regular was chosen from many others.
And then a placement of words was chosen and that’s where I had an issue that needed fixing…

Logo development process

There was a weird space that made the upper words (JAZZ) seem to be not balancing and somehow almost falling.

Logo development process

Putting this block seemed to be working but I needed a reason for putting the block there. Obviously the reason is clear that it provides a visual anchor and maintains balance within the overall composition.

Logo development process

So, I thought of the jazz music being more of a music of melodies, some high and some low. Jazz is spontaneous. Also music or sound is represented with such blocks moving up and down. I also thought of this instrument (trumpet) how it is being played (the pressing of the valves) resonates with the up and down move portrayed in the logo.

Logo in full color

Knowing Jazz to be spontaneous, I believe it’s music that is multicoloured. Not only because it is spontaneous but it is also enjoyed by different races and it kept evolving since it started to suit the generation it is in. Above all, the colours are bright and represents the youth and energy of the music.

Visual Graphics

I didn’t want to move away from the logo’s blockie look, so I decided to use geometric shapes in the branding of the entire Festival. I broke some letters into pieces and used those blocks to do something interesting, young and vibe.

Project Description

Find a local business that does not have design work or a strong visual identity and help them develop a cohesive brand presence.

Problem Statement

Lifeskills Hub does not have design work. The only thing that they have is a logo that they use on their mechanize. The logo itself is not so great, has lot of inconsistency and does not represent Lifeskills Hub in the best way. Touchpoints are not so many, no functional website or socials.

Deliverables

1. Logo - designing a logo that looks young, fresh and professional to fit their target audience.
2. Identity systems - these are basic graphics, images, colours, and typography that people will use to easily and instantly identify them with.
3. Marketing and promotional asserts - these include posters, flyers, banners and a website. I want them to be consitent all across in terms of the herarchy of information, colours used, graphic treatment and the pictures they use as well as the tone and voice used. These will outline what LifeSkills Hub is about, bringing awareness to people and helping them see how they can benefit from it.
4. Website - this replaces the system they have of having to text the CEO when you have to apply or if you need their services. It will provide you with information and then take you through the application process.
5. Motion graphics - they say the most successful brands are not static but fluid.

Who We Are

LifeSkills Hub operates as a bridge between unemployment and employment, delivering accredited and non-accredited training programmes, work readiness initiatives, learnership placements, internships, recruitment services, and business compliance support.

Moodboard

Geared towards youthfulness, fun and modernity. Has a touch of handwritten scripts to add that “human” touch. Also an inclusion of features of hand circled words or hand drawn direction pointers to give it that engaging playfulness and fun feel. Chose these colours because they are youthful and not too serious, they have a vibe fun feeling as well.

Logo Concepts

All the ideas I had concerning the logo design had to do with concepts of growth, direction, central area of activity, and connection. These originated from the meannig of HUB and what LifeSkills Hub is all about. I mapped out all the words and started thinking of how to visualize them.

Chosen logo

This was the chosen concept. It is made from the letters LSH (LifeSkills Hub). You can say well, I don’t see how it communicate the ideas spoken of. A hub is a central area of activity or a central device that connects multiple devices. To convey connection - I made use of the letters and combined all of them to become one. These then were converted into a more abstract shape where the letters were made to be negative spaces in between the blocks. The negative space feel like a way that controls the eys how to move which then directs the eye to the name. These communicate connection and direction. Enhousing some of the LifeSkills Hub purposes which is to give people skills that will allow them to be equipped for their intended course of work and also get them work ready - setting them on the right path.

Logo in full colour

Went for more vibrant and lively colours. The colours bring excitement, catches attention and sparks awareness. Balanced with a dark blue to bring in that feel of safety and professionalism.

Visual Design

The same visuals in the logo were then used for the business card design and other visual materials for the Lifeskills Hub.
